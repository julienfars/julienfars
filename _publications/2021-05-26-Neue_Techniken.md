Inherited retinal disorders involving cone dysfunction can cause marked visual impairment and significant color vision abnormalities despite relatively unremarkable fundus findings. Examples include autosomal-dominant occult macular dystrophy (RP1L1 gene) and X-linked retinitis pigmentosa (RPGR gene)—the latter also occurring in heterozygous female carriers. New diagnostic methods make it possible to quantify the extent of color vision deficiency.
